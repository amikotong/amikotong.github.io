Developing a Vancouver-based e-commerce platform: offering a cohesive online destination for shopping, auctions, and trading, enhancing local commerce and convenience.

Progress

2 months hybrid design work completed
currently under development by the programmers
Contribution

UI Designe and Prototype, initially building the platform's UI and design system
Collaboration

The client team: CEO, Investors, Executive Manager, Dev Lead, Brand Designer
Kick off meeting
The client team needed an MVP ( minimum viable product ) version e-commerce website for trial runs. They also required reusable UI components ( a design system ). The client expected a user interface that would appeal to North American users, with a simple and clean aesthetic. They required the design and prototyping to be completed within two months.
Challenge 01: The project needs to be completed under a tight deadline.
Condense to essentials
After organizing the sitemap, we realized that the website had too much content to complete all at once. I suggested minimizing the workload and focusing on the main functionalities first. Despite this, we still found the workload daunting. After discussion, we decided to prioritize tasks and complete them in stages. We focused on simpler and less ambiguous features first (such as landing pages, shops, auctions, trades, etc.).
Challenge 02: The client proposed some innovative features for which I had few examples to reference.
Rapid prototype and iterate
To ensure smooth and rapid design completion, I established an effective collaboration routine with the team: discussing the functions, confirming wireframes, presenting prototypes, and refine.
Design system
After finalizing the core functionalities and style, I adopted the atomic design methodology to replicate UI elements for similar features with minimal adjustments. This approach enabled the creation of a robust design system, ensuring reusability and consistency throughout the project.
In this project, I was responsible for initially building the platform's user interface and the first design system. This involved communication, discussion, design, and presenting prototypes, as well as coordinating with developers.
The developers requested a design system that could be quickly assembled, with interface blocks that could be reused or reassembled, similar to building a burger at McDonald's.
To facilitate clearer communication and ensure the completeness of the website structure, I led the team in discussing and constructing a sitemap. Through this process, I gained insight into the team's needs, brand direction, and their definition of similar products.
